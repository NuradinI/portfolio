Learned from Bayyinah

In hadith Qudsi the prophet paraphrases what Allah said. The message is from Allah but he put the words together. The quran is a word chosen from Allah. “I divided the prayer in two halves between myself and my slaves” the rest of the hadith starts with the fatiha. And then he says that when the slave says alhamdulillah Allah says my slave has just praised me. So we will start with after bismillah.(There is a major difference in opinions within the schools of thought).

اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ رَبِّ الْعٰلَمِیْنَۙ

Alhamdulilah means praise and thanks belongs to Allah. Nouman ali khan puts two words together, Madih(praise) and shukur(thanks) to communicate this. Madih can be false, it can also be done to living and nonliving things. Hamd(praise) is only done for the living, hamd has to be sincere. You say thank you when someone does something to you. In thanks you had to recognize something for you has been done. Gratitude is a reaction, hamd isn't a reaction. Hamd is independent. How come Allah didnt say "Praise Allah", it then becomes a order from god, with an order there are two options, either they do it or they don't. The ball isn't in your court, it's in the other person's court. If Allah said praise Allah that means we are responsible. But Allah didn't say that because that would make it dependent on us whether we gave him praise or not.

اَلْحَمْدُ لِلّٰهِ رَبِّ الْعٰلَمِیْنَۙ

We are now on rab. Which has five meanings from what I know(The owner, the one in charge, the caretaker, the giver of gifts, the one who makes sure it does not die, or the maintainer), but the main meaning is master. A master that has those qualities. This is now talking about the relationship that we have with Allah. For example, in marriage there are two parties, husband and wife, in class there are two parties, teacher and students. So if Allah is master we are slave. If he is caretaker we are the ones being taken care of and so on with the other meanings.

The word rab is the beginning of our relationship with Allah. The entire quran fundamentally revolves around the idea that accept Allah as master and accept yourself as slave. What Allah does with his property is up to him and it is justified. Because we are property to him. No master let you keep going with mistakes, worst case they’ll punish but at the best case they’ll remove your privileges. But even as we keep sinning and keep making mistakes against our master he still continues to give his gifts and blessings. A master is also not an employer. The point Nouman is giving is that we are slaves every hour of our days. Once you're employed you only do the certain things you are told to do for your shift. Meaning that when you're done with your shift you don't have to do anything they tell you too. Also you only do what you're employed for if you're an accountant the employer can't tell you to wash the window. Contrary to that a master can ask for anything from a slave and the slave has to abide regardless of what the master asks.

الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

Second Ayah. Rough translation is the most graceful, the most merciful. Both of these mean the most merciful. Mercy in the arab language means your taken care of in every angle. When two words in arabic are put next to each other they mean two different things. What makes these different? In Ar-rahman by definition it's something extreme. It has three qualities, extreme mercy, mercy taking place right now. Just because you call someone merciful does not mean your being merciful at that moment. Ar-rahman means that this person is being extremely merciful right now. The next one is transience. Meaning it is temporary. But you may do something that will take away his mercy. May Allah protect us from that ameen.

The two qualities of Ar-raheem is that it is permanent, and is not necessarily happening right now. If i say a person is strong that does not mean they are using their strength right now. But that does not diminish their strength just cause they are not using it at that moment. But before we said Ar-raheem is not always permanent. But now this word says it is permanent. These two definitions cover Ar-rahman in what it is missing when talking about Allah’s mercy. Ar-rahman said it is not permanent and Ar-raheem said it is, Ar-rahman said it is happening at all times and Ar-raheem said it is not necessarily happening right now. All possible angles of mercy are being covered by these two words.

Ar-rahman and Ar-raheem cover the immediate and the future. Nouman gave the analogy of an employee living paycheck to paycheck. And he is waiting for his boss to give the money to him but he needs it by 5:00 and it is 4:45. Another employee sees him stressing and says don't stress he is reliable. The employee replies I know he is reliable. I just wish he was being reliable right now. But as soon as the employee gets his paycheck and pays for all of which he is responsible for he begins to start worrying about the future and what's to come. Allah said Ar-rahman first one of the three qualities was that it is happening right now. The employee who got the paycheck from his boss and handled his responsibilities begins to plan and worry about the future to see if he’ll be able to sustain those responsibilities. One of Ar-raheem qualities was that it is not necessarily happening right now. Meaning that Allah’s mercy may not be happening right now but that does not mean because it is not happening right now it will not happen later. This covers the future. Similar to how an employee living paycheck to paycheck may not be getting ready to pay this month's rent does not mean he does not have the capability to do so.

مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ

The owner/king of the day of judgment. There are two ways the say malik and the two different ways have two different meanings. King and owner. On the day of judgment everything will be taken into account. You don't call yourself a king over something small, but you'll call yourself the owner of something small. Ex I’ll call myself the owner of this water bottle not the king, and if I was the king of a nation I wouldn't call myself the owner of the nation but the king. So in terms of the day of judgment both big and small things are going to be accounted for so Allah is both owner and king, covering both big and small. Allah said he is the owner of the DAY of judgment, not just judgment. So why did Allah call himself the owner of that day. An analogy is given by Nouman, he says, if I have a bag and I say i'm the owner of this bag, that would mean that I also own the contents inside this bag logically. If Allah says he is the owner of that day it means that since he owns the day as well everything that happens within that day.

إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ

In this ayah we declare that we are going to enslave ourselves to Allah because of the first ayah regarding rab, him being the caretaker, master, the one keeping us alive and so on. If that is not enough then it is because he is Ar-rahman, and if that isn't enough it is because he is Ar-Raheem, and if that is not enough then be wary because he is the malik of the day of judgment. And in this ayah we declare submission. In this ayah it is phrased as if we are talking to him not about him. Allah begins fatiha by telling himself about us and then now we begin to reply back as if we are conversing with Allah. In doing this we enslave ourselves to Allah. Nouman goes on to talk about how when we do this it is a declaration of complete dependence on Allah, and in doing that we truly become free. Either people are enslaved to creation or a creator and once you enslave yourself to creation you've taken upon an imperfect finite master. All human beings are slaves to something but we have the free will to choose what we wish to enslave ourselves to and in this ayah we choose Allah swt.

In doing this we gain independence from everything else, those who do not take upon Allah as they’re master are enslaved to multiple other masters such as desires, temptations, ideologies, family, peer pressure, culture etc. This ayah means only you who we worship and enslaves ourselves to. In this ayah iyakanastaeen means we are asking Allah for help. This word is defined as asking for help when you're struggling on something that you’ve already attempted and tried. Ibrahim As was thrown into the fire first and then it became cool, the muslims went to the battle of Badr and then the angels helped them. So do not ask for help if you haven’t tried. So you best not be lying to Allah when you stand in front of Allah and read this ayah, because with this you’ve claimed that you already made the effort, and claiming that you're struggling and you're the only one who I will turn to. If it is time to pray one of the daily prayers and you are rushing to finishing the salah, you stand in front of Allah and say I’m trying so hard Allah to be your slave just help me I’m putting in the effort, that would be a lie to not only yourself but Allah as well. A major insult to Allah. What Allah wants is primary what we want is secondary that is why we declare our dependence and ourselves to Allah and then ask for help. Which is why the format of this ayah was worded with what we owe Allah first and then what we want from Allah second. In the ayah we did not say what we need help with. There are a multitude of reasons why Allah did not put it in there for us but Nouman gives 3. The first one is that Allah already knows what you need help with. The second one is the list can be so long that you just say “help”, and the third thing is you're really desperate so you just cry out for help.

اهدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ

When we ask for help we need to specify what kind of help we want and that is what this ayah is specifying, Allah is telling us that if we ask for this kind of help this will then cover all the other things we need help with and that is guidance. Now with surah Fatiha we read this chapter every single day 17 times a day mandatory not optional. This would mean we ask for guidance over and over again. If someone is dying of thirst and asks someone for water, and he does not receive the water he's going to ask again and again because he’s desperate. The one who asks for something over and over again is desperate, and we ask for guidance between rakats. The person who asks is also the one in need. So we both need and are extremely desperate for Allah’s guidance.

صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلاَ الضَّالِّينَ

In this ayah we are asking Allah to make us of a certain group of people. The first half of is speaking of wanting to be with those who are favoured and the second half is us asking Allah not to make us of those who received anger. When we are asking for favour from Allah he is mentioned, but in the other half Allah is not mentioned. The second half is us asking Allah to not make us upon those who you've hurled your anger upon. Allah is so angry at them he does not even want to be mentioned with them. A verb was used in the first half of this ayah, and a noun was used in the later half of this ayah. When using a verb it is not always there, but using a noun implies that it is always there. Meaning that the people that we should look out for are always going to be there(the ones who have got Allah's anger hurled upon them), and the people who have recieved Allah's favour will not alway be there. When doing wrong there are two types of people, people who know better and those who didn't know better. In the second half of the ayah these two types of people are described, adDaleen is those who didn't know, and maqdoobi is those who did. Regardless both will still get a punishment for it. Ignorance is not a justifiable plea of innocence. So we ask Allah to not make us of those who knew better or did not know ameen.
